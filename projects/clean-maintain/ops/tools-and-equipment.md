## Phase 2 Additions (Once You Have 3+ Regular Clients)

### Air Compressor

**Use for:** Blowing dust out of flywheel cages — faster than vacuuming alone.

**Best practice:** Use compressed air first to dislodge dust, then vacuum it up. Blow + vacuum combo is faster and cleaner than vacuum alone.

**Limitations:**
- Disperses dust into the gym air — schedule visits before gym opens or when it's quiet
- Not a replacement for the vacuum; use them together

**Options:**
- Small portable compressor: €40-80 (cost-effective for regular work)
- Cans of compressed air (Dust-Off type): €8-12/can (cheaper to start, not long-term viable)

**Verdict: Nice to have once you're doing volume. A shop vac alone is fine for early clients.**

---

### Steam Cleaner

**Use for:**
- SkiErg floor platform (rubber/plastic base — no moving parts)
- Assault Bike footrests and frame (avoiding console + drivetrain)
- Rubber gym mats, benches, general floor areas
- Barbells/dumbbells before re-oiling

**Do NOT use on:**
- PM5 monitors or any electronics — steam damages electronics
- Chain or bearings — strips lubrication, can cause corrosion
- Inside flywheel cage — moisture risk on internal metal parts
- Concept2 microfiber suede grips

**Why the restriction:** Concept2 explicitly says no direct liquid spray on equipment. High-pressure steam carries the same risk — moisture can enter the flywheel housing and cause internal corrosion over time.

**Cost:** €50-150 for a decent unit.

**Verdict: Not for the ergs themselves. Useful only if you expand into floor and mat cleaning as a Phase 3 add-on. Not needed to start.**

## What to NEVER Use on Concept2 Equipment

- WD-40 on chains (solvent — strips lubrication, attracts dirt)
- Alcohol on microfiber suede grips (degrades adhesive, causes peeling)
- Direct spray of any liquid on monitors/electronics
- Undiluted bleach on monorail
- Steam directly on flywheel housing or electronics
- Coarse abrasives on any surfaces
- Mineral acids
